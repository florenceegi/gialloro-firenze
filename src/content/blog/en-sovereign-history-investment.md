The **Gold Sovereign** is the longest-running bullion coin in the world. Struck by the Royal Mint since 1817, it has survived four reigns, two world wars and the end of the gold standard, remaining a recognised store of value throughout. Unlike bars, it is **divisible**: you can sell one coin at a time without unwinding the entire position.

## Weight, purity, dimensions

- **Total weight**: 7.98 g
- **Fine gold (22kt)**: 7.32 g
- **Diameter**: 22.05 mm
- **Thickness**: 1.52 mm
- **Purity**: 916.7/1000 (22 carats)

The remaining alloy is copper, which gives the coin its characteristic reddish tone and hardness. Thanks to that copper content, the Sovereign has withstood 200 years of circulation without damage.

## Four monarchs, four centuries

**Queen Victoria (1837-1901)** — the longest series. Three portraits: *Young Head*, *Jubilee Head*, *Old Head*. Early vintages are the most sought-after by collectors.

**Edward VII (1902-1910)** — nine vintages, design by George William de Saulles. Saint George on horseback remains the most recognisable reverse.

**George V (1911-1936)** — last issue in regular circulation. After 1931 the UK abandoned the gold standard and the Sovereign became strictly an investment coin.

**Elizabeth II (1957-2022)** — reissued as bullion in 1957. Five different portraits accompany the UK's longest reign.
