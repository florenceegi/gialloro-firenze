Appraising gold takes **15 minutes on average** in store. Not hours, not days: fifteen minutes with you present, everything under your eyes. Here's how we work.

## 1. Metal identification

First we read the **hallmark**. Legal Italian gold carries a stamp with purity in thousandths (750 = 18kt, 585 = 14kt, 375 = 9kt) and a maker identification number. If the hallmark is missing or illegible, we use the **touchstone** with acid reagents: we gently scratch the piece on a black stone, then apply the acid specific to each purity. The colour of the reaction confirms the carat.

## 2. Weighing on a certified scale

The scale is **CE-approved class II**, with calibration certificate renewed every two years. We weigh to the hundredth of a gram. If the jewel contains non-removable stones, their weight is deducted using density tables.

## 3. London Gold Fixing calculation

Every morning at 10:30 (and 15:00 UK time) the **London Gold Fixing** publishes the price of 24kt gold per gram. From that number, multiplied by the purity ratio, we get the market value of pure gold contained in your piece. Example: 1 gram of 18kt = 750/1000 × 24kt price.

## 4. Written offer

We present a **transparent document** with: weight, purity, daily quote, commercial margin (typically between 15 and 20%), final value. No pressure to sign. If you accept, we pay by traceable bank transfer the same day — OAM regulatory requirement. If you don't, the piece goes back to you untouched.

## What to bring to the store

- Valid ID
- Tax code (codice fiscale)
- Original purchase receipt if available (not mandatory)
- The gold to be appraised — even broken, unpaired, unwearable
